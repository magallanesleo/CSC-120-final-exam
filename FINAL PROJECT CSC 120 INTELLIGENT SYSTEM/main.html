( Flower Image classification )

In this context, we propose a flower classification approach based on a convolutional neural network. We have applied a transfer learning approach for the classification of flowers. We have used convolution neural network architecture for the extraction of features. As we wanted to classify flowers into 17 different so we have used 5 neurons in the final dense layer of convolution neural network architecture with the use of activation function. Results show that we have 5 classified flowers which are 734 Daisy, 734 Dandelion, 734 Roses, 734 Sunflowers, and 734 Tulips with a validation accuracy of 0.6362 and training accuracy of 100%. Flower classification is a challenging task due to the wide range of flower species, which have a similar shape, appearance, or surrounding objects such as leaves and grass.
